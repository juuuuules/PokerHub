Bluffing

Bluffing is bet a large amount on a weak hand in order to make oppenents think you have a strong hand. When you bluff, you are trying to convince your oppents to fold out of fear that you have a better hand then them, even though you don't. Here are some resources to learn more:

Tells

Tells are specific actions that players repeat that seem to indicate the nature of their hand. Things like a sigh, a slight smile, or even fiddling with chips can give away specific information about a players hand. This strategy often requires you to know the other players well. Here are some resources to learn more:
Links

Hand Reading

Hand Reading is trying to assign a player a range of hands that you think they likely fall into based on their actions. By using tells, you can estimate the strength of a players hand, and play accordingly. Here are some resources to learn more:
Links

Advanced

Ranges

Ranges are a range of hands you are going to play a certain way. Depending on what position you are, and your play style, there are a certain set of hands you will call, others you will bet, and some you will fold. A range chart makes this decision making easy, as it displays all of the possible cards combinations and how to play for each of them. You can also choose a range chart catered to your play style, depending on how risky you like to play. Here are some resources to learn more:
Links

3-Betting

A 3-bet is when after someone raises from the intial bet, another player re-raises. In a game, you only want to do this very specific circumstances, like when you have a very good hand, or when you are trying to bluff that you have a very good hand.
